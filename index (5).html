How I Design

A clear process for every project.

Four principles that guide every design decision — from discovery to delivery.

01

Identify Real User Friction

I begin with genuine curiosity — interviewing users, observing behaviour, and mapping pain points before drawing a single wireframe. Good design starts with honest listening.

02

Simplify Before Adding Features

Complexity is the enemy of usability. I strip interactions to their essence, removing noise before considering additions — because clarity is always the better feature.

03

Design for Behaviour and Clarity

I design with how people actually think and act — not how we wish they would. Every layout, label, and interaction is grounded in real-world behaviour and cognitive patterns.

04

Validate with User Actions and Metrics

Opinions aren't evidence. I test prototypes with real users, track completion rates, and let behaviour — not assumptions — determine whether a design is truly working.

Wellness · Mobile App

GentleFit

A low-impact fitness app designed for users who feel intimidated by conventional workout platforms — making movement feel achievable, not overwhelming.

UX Research Prototyping UI Design

Problem Statement

The Challenge

First-time fitness app users felt overwhelmed by progress tracking, complex navigation, and unrealistic expectations — leading to early drop-off.

Target Users

Who It's For

  • Beginners new to fitness apps
  • Low-to-moderate activity levels
  • People recovering from injury
  • Ages 25–45, mixed digital literacy

Research Insights

What I Found

  • Users wanted encouragement, not judgment
  • Complex dashboards created anxiety
  • Short, flexible sessions preferred
  • Visual progress > numeric tracking

Design Goals

What I Set Out to Do

  • Reduce onboarding to under 3 steps
  • Make progress feel gentle & visible
  • Tone — supportive, not competitive
  • Design for low-distraction sessions

Key Design Decisions

Replaced numeric progress bars with illustrated milestone moments. Used a calm colour palette with micro-animations on workout completion. Onboarding reduced to a 2-step preference quiz with no account wall.

Outcome & Learnings

Usability tests showed users completed full sessions without dropping off. Tone-first approach was consistently praised.

Food & Lifestyle · Mobile App

CookedUp

A recipe discovery app that reduces decision fatigue for home cooks — surfacing the right recipe based on what's already in your kitchen.

User Flows Interaction Design Figma

Problem Statement

The Challenge

Home cooks waste time scrolling irrelevant recipes. Apps suggest meals without considering ingredients on hand, causing frustration and food waste.

Target Users

Who It's For

  • Busy working adults cooking at home
  • Budget-conscious meal planners
  • Cooks 3–5 times per week
  • Low-to-moderate cooking confidence

Research Insights

What I Found

  • "What can I make now?" was #1 query
  • Ingredient input felt like a chore
  • Visual cards drove more engagement
  • Users opened apps out of habit

Design Goals

What I Set Out to Do

  • Ingredient input under 30 seconds
  • Surface relevant results immediately
  • Scannable, visual recipe browsing
  • Fewer steps from open to cooking

Key Design Decisions

Ingredient input uses a fast-tag system — type one letter, tap to confirm. Recipes ranked by match percentage. A "cook now" shortcut bypasses browse entirely.

Outcome & Learnings

Users reached recipe selection in under 45 seconds. Tag input system was the most-liked feature.

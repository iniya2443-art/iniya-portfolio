About Me

From business thinking to human-centered design.

I design clear, structured digital products that guide users with confidence. My business background gives me an edge in understanding product goals and stakeholder needs.

How I Design

A clear process for every project.

Four principles that guide every design decision — from discovery to delivery.

01

Identify Real User Friction

I begin with genuine curiosity — interviewing users and mapping pain points before drawing a single wireframe.

02

Simplify Before Adding Features

Complexity is the enemy of usability. I strip interactions to their essence — because clarity is always the better feature.

03

Design for Behaviour and Clarity

Every layout, label, and interaction is grounded in real-world behaviour and cognitive patterns.

04

Validate with User Actions and Metrics

I test prototypes with real users and let behaviour — not assumptions — determine whether a design is working.

Wellness · Mobile App

GentleFit

A low-impact fitness app for beginners — mood-based routines that adapt to your daily energy.

UX ResearchPrototypingUI Design

Problem Statement

The Challenge

First-time fitness app users felt overwhelmed by complex navigation and unrealistic expectations.

Target Users

Who It's For

  • Beginners new to fitness apps
  • Low-to-moderate activity levels
  • Ages 25–45

Research Insights

What I Found

  • Users wanted encouragement
  • Dashboards created anxiety
  • Visual progress preferred

Design Goals

What I Set Out to Do

  • Reduce onboarding to 3 steps
  • Supportive, not competitive tone
  • Mood-based session design

Key Design Decisions

Replaced numeric progress bars with illustrated milestones. Onboarding reduced to a 2-step quiz with no account wall.

Outcome & Learnings

Users completed full sessions without dropping off. Tone-first approach was consistently praised.

↑ Session completion↓ Drop-offHigh satisfaction

Food & Lifestyle · Mobile App

CookedUp

A Tamil Nadu-special recipe discovery app — personalized meal plans and food delivery in one clean UI.

User FlowsInteraction DesignFigma

Problem Statement

The Challenge

Home cooks waste time scrolling irrelevant recipes without considering ingredients on hand.

Target Users

Who It's For

  • Busy working adults
  • Budget-conscious planners
  • Cooks 3–5 times/week

Research Insights

What I Found

  • "What can I make now?" #1 query
  • Ingredient input felt like a chore
  • Visual cards drove more engagement

Design Goals

What I Set Out to Do

  • Ingredient input under 30 seconds
  • Surface relevant results fast
  • Fewer steps to start cooking

Key Design Decisions

Fast-tag ingredient system — type one letter, tap to confirm. Recipes ranked by match percentage.

Outcome & Learnings

Users reached recipe selection in under 45 seconds. Tag input was the most-liked feature.

45s avg to recipe↑ Return usageTop-rated input
